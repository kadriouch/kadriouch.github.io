01 — About

Background

With a Ph.D. in Applied Mathematics and over a decade of experience teaching college-level mathematics, I bring a unique perspective to computer science — one grounded in analytical rigor and a deep commitment to clear problem-solving. I am currently completing a B.S. in Computer Science at the University of the People while teaching at multiple institutions including Baruch College.

My research computing background began with Fortran simulations and Linux servers during graduate school, giving me a foundation that spans from low-level systems to modern full-stack development. I am passionate about leveraging computational tools to solve complex problems and making technical concepts accessible to diverse learners.

I am multilingual and draw on that ability to support students from varied backgrounds. Whether debugging a bash script, designing a database schema, or proving a theorem, I approach every challenge with the same methodical precision and curiosity.
